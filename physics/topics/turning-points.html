Discovery of the Electron

Thermionic emission and cathode rays

In 1897 J.J. Thomson showed that cathode rays were streams of negatively charged particles (electrons) by deflecting them with electric and magnetic fields and measuring their charge-to-mass ratio.

Millikan's oil drop experiment

Millikan (1909) measured the charge on individual oil droplets by balancing gravitational and electric forces. He showed charge is always a multiple of a fundamental value $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\ \text{C}$.

$$\text{At terminal velocity: } qE = mg + 6\pi\eta r v$$

Electric force upward balances weight and Stokes drag. When field off: $mg = 6\pi\eta r v_0$ determines $r$, hence $m = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \rho$.

Thomson's $e/m$ measurement

In Thomson's crossed-field experiment, electric and magnetic fields are balanced so the beam is undeflected: $qE = qvB$, giving $v = E/B$. Then the magnetic field alone deflects the beam in a circle: $qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$, so:

$$\frac{e}{m} = \frac{E}{B^2 r}$$

Thomson found $e/m \approx 1.76 \times 10^{11}\ \text{C kg}^{-1}$.

Wave–Particle Duality

The photoelectric effect

When light above a threshold frequency hits a metal surface, electrons are emitted immediately. This could not be explained by wave theory — Einstein (1905) explained it by proposing light consists of photons:

$$hf = \phi + E_{k,\max} \qquad E_{k,\max} = h(f - f_0)$$

$h$ = Planck's constant, $f$ = frequency, $\phi = hf_0$ = work function, $f_0$ = threshold frequency, $E_{k,\max}$ = maximum KE of emitted electrons.

Why wave theory fails Wave theory predicts: (1) any frequency should eventually eject electrons if intensity is high enough; (2) a time delay before emission as energy builds up. Both predictions are wrong — photoelectric emission is immediate and only occurs above $f_0$ regardless of intensity.

De Broglie wavelength

If light (waves) can behave as particles, de Broglie proposed that particles can also behave as waves:

$$\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$$

Confirmed by electron diffraction — electrons passing through a crystal lattice produce a diffraction pattern, proving they have wave properties.

Electron microscopes

Electrons accelerated through a voltage $V$ gain kinetic energy $eV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$. Their de Broglie wavelength is much smaller than visible light, allowing electron microscopes to resolve detail far beyond optical microscopes.

▶ Worked Example

An electron is accelerated through 5000 V. Calculate its de Broglie wavelength. ($m_e = 9.11 \times 10^{-31}$ kg)

1
KE gained: $eV = 1.6\times10^{-19} \times 5000 = 8.0\times10^{-16}\ \text{J}$
2
$v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 8.0\times10^{-16}}{9.11\times10^{-31}}} = 4.19\times10^7\ \text{m s}^{-1}$
3
$\lambda = \frac{h}{mv} = \frac{6.63\times10^{-34}}{9.11\times10^{-31} \times 4.19\times10^7} = 1.74\times10^{-11}\ \text{m}$ (0.017 nm)

Special Relativity

Einstein's special relativity (1905) is built on two postulates:

  1. The laws of physics are the same in all inertial (non-accelerating) frames of reference.
  2. The speed of light in a vacuum is the same for all observers, regardless of the motion of the source.

Time dilation

A clock moving relative to an observer runs slow — moving clocks tick slower than stationary ones:

$$t = \frac{t_0}{\sqrt{1 - v^2/c^2}} = \gamma t_0 \qquad \text{where } \gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}$$

$t_0$ = proper time (measured in the rest frame), $t$ = dilated time measured by the moving observer, $\gamma \geq 1$.

Length contraction

An object moving relative to an observer appears shorter in the direction of motion:

$$l = \frac{l_0}{\gamma} = l_0\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}$$

$l_0$ = proper length (rest frame), $l$ = contracted length. Only the dimension parallel to motion contracts.

Relativistic mass–energy

$$E = \gamma m_0 c^2 \qquad E_0 = m_0 c^2 \qquad E_k = (\gamma - 1)m_0 c^2$$

$m_0$ = rest mass, $E_0$ = rest energy, $E_k$ = relativistic kinetic energy. At low speeds $E_k \approx \frac{1}{2}m_0v^2$.

Evidence for Special Relativity
  • Muon decay: cosmic ray muons (created 10 km up) reach Earth's surface despite their short half-life — only explained by time dilation in their rest frame / length contraction in ours
  • GPS satellites: clocks on GPS satellites run fast (special relativity) and slow (general relativity); corrections are essential for accuracy
  • Particle accelerators: particles approaching $c$ require increasing energy but cannot reach $c$ — consistent with $E = \gamma m_0 c^2$
